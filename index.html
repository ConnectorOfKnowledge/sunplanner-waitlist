Calibration

Why the numbers are actually right

Generic solar apps start from a clean-sky average and call it a forecast. SunPlanner starts from your actual setup. You enter what your system produces, and the forecast adjusts to match your rig. On the compass side, metal and battery banks shift the magnetometer reading by 10 to 30 degrees. SunPlanner calibrates that too, so sun-finding stays accurate in the places you actually use it.

What it does

Forecast

Built around your setup

Give it your panel specs and a location, and you get a day-by-day production forecast. Your current spot, your cabin, your mooring, or anywhere you're considering. Not a regional average.

Anywhere

Forecast for any location

Save spots or tap the map. Useful before you decide where to set up, or just to see what tomorrow looks like from where you are now.

Sun Finder

Find the sun for your exact spot

Point your phone and see where the sun rises, how high it gets, and when it sets. Helpful when trees, a hill, or a building is cutting your production day short.
